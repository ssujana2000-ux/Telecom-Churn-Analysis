## Business Statement
    The objective of this analysis is to understand customer plan usage, identify key drivers of churn and quantify revenue impact to improve customer retention.

##	Data Cleaning and EDA
1.	The dataset was imported into Power BI, data types were corrected, and no missing values were found in key churn, tenure, or revenue fields.
2.	Customer status and churn categories were validated, and non-essential geographic data was retained only for potential future use.
3.	The primary churn drivers are better competitor offers and customer dissatisfaction, including service and support-related issues.
4.	Customer churn is highest within the first 6 months, while long-tenure customers show stronger retention.
5.	The dataset represents approximately 21M in total revenue, with churned customers contributing ~17%, indicating a significant revenue impact.

The dashboard tracks key telecom churn metrics including total customers, churn rate, churned customers, average tenure, and revenue impact. Customer behavior is analyzed across tenure groups, contract types, age segments, offers, and churn reasons to identify high-risk segments and revenue drivers.

ğŸ” Business Insights Summary

Customer churn is highest among early-tenure customers and those on month-to-month contracts. Competitor-related reasons are the primary drivers of churn, often linked to pricing, speed, and device offerings. Customers requesting refunds show higher churn risk, while retention offers significantly reduce churn. A small group of high-value customers contributes to a large share of revenue loss, highlighting the need for targeted retention strategies.
